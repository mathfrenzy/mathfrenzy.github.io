Our Past Events

V3SSEL was founded with the goal of connecting the youth of our community with our government, and introducing them to new career possibilities. Below our some of our most memorable events that have helped us work towards this goal.

Virtual Speakership Event

Ambassador Frederick Barton

Former Assistant Secretary of State Frederick Barton speaks on his experiences as a diplomat and provides insight on how to run a successful political campaign.

Ambassador Barton’s career as a public servant is one to be admired. He started his career in government as a young candidate for Congress in Maine and rose to become a leader in the State Department who worked closely with Secretary of State Hillary Clinton as the founder of the Bureau of Conflict and Stabilization Operations. Along the way, he worked as a Co-Director at the Center for Strategic and International Studies and served as the US Ambassador to the Economic and Social Council of the United Nations where he represented the United States on the world stage on key issues from climate change to human rights. He eventually became the Assistant Secretary of State under Barack Obama from 2011-2014. Currently, he is working as a lecturer and Co-Director of Ullman Fellowship at Princeton University and recently released a groundbreaking book on America’s global interventions called Peace Works, which can be found on Amazon.

John Schroder

Mr. Schroder speaks on his experiences as a former State Department Official, and gives his advice to the next generation of leaders.

John Schroder is an international civil servant and business executive. Mr. Schroder served as a Lead Foreign Affairs Officer at the US Department of State’s Bureau of Conflict and Stabilization Operations (CSO). He served as a Protection Officer at the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) in Sri Lanka, where he chaired the inter-agency Advocacy Task Force of the Protection Working Group and planned the post-war return of 120,000 Sri Lankan refugees from India. As Repatriation Officer at UNHCR in Afghanistan, he helped facilitate the voluntary repatriation of some 350,000 Afghan refugees from Pakistan and Iran over a two-year period. Following his work with the State Department, he co-founded and led Steelhead Global Advisors, a Seattle-based international business advisory firm. He was a member of the founding team at LAVLE, a Japan-US joint venture company that develops and supplies advanced battery technologies for the maritime, defense, rail, and renewable energy markets.

Angelina Nastasi

Ms. Nastasi gives her opinion on activism and youth involvement in our nation while discussing a variety of other topics. Her views and opinions do not reflect, nor are associated with Amnesty International.

Ms. Nastasi is currently an activist leader and field organizer within Amnesty International. As a field organizer, she developed and implemented effective grassroots community organizing strategies in New York, New Jersey, and Pennsylvania on key human rights issues from gun violence to the conditions at detention centers at the southern border. Additionally, she has worked with over 80 student groups in the northeast and has coordinated joint action with those groups on various issues and recently shifted to working with Amnesty International student groups throughout the south. Her experiences provide a truly unique insight into the importance of youth activism and involvement.

Senator Joseph Cryan

Senator Cryan discusses how the youth can get involved in their community during this time, and provides insight on how the government is handling the COVID-19 pandemic, providing important details to when our nation might fully recover realistically.

State Senator Joseph Cryan is an American Democratic politician who represents the 20th Legislative District. Elected to the General Assembly in 2002, he served as an Assemblyman for 12 years and temporarily served as Majority leader from 2010 - 2012. In 2012 he was elected into the state senate and serves as the Vice Chair of both the Commerce and Law and Public Safety. Additionally, he serves as the Chair of the N.J. Democratic State Committee.

Vanessa Kulick

Ms. Kulick speaks on her experiences as a former State Department Official, describing the different places she worked and visited, as well as her experiences in conflict resolution with nations on the verge of Civil War.

Vanessa Kulick is a foreign affairs specialist with nearly a decade of experience with the State Department. Starting her career at the Center for Strategic and International Studies she worked as a research consultant. Later, she began to work as a Security Analyst for the World Bank. In 2011 she began to serve in the US State Department as a foreign affairs officer, where she became Central African Lead and undertook analysis, oversaw programming and supported U.S. strategy to reduce risk of destabilizing violence in Central Africa. Currently, Ms. Kulick works at her flower farm called Indigo Page Flower Farm and consults for the private sector.

Professor Brian Potter

Professor Pottter speaks on a wide range of topics from foreign policy to the impact of the COVID-19 Pandmeic on our country.

Professor Brian Potter teaches political economy and international relations with a focus on Latin America at The College of New Jersey. After earning his Ph.D. in Political Science from the University of California, Los Angeles, Dr. Potter taught at Tulane University before coming to TCNJ in 2004. His work on environmental and economic policy has appeared in Latin American Perspectives, the Canadian Journal of Political Science, Energy and Development, Environmental Politics and as chapters in edited volumes.

Aside from politics, Mr. Houghtaling is an accomplished electrician who has proudly been a member of the International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers Local 400 for 40 years. He also serves on the Telecommunications and Utilities Committee and the Labor Committee. His extensive experience does not end there; Mr. Houghtaling serves as the Chair of the Agriculture and Natural Resources Committee and as the Vice-Chair of the Oversight, Reform and Federal Relations Committee.
